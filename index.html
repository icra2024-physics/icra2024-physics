Physics-driven Methods in Robot Planning and Control

ICRA 2024 Workshop

Overview

Motion planning and control are pivotal components in robotics, where researchers continue to strive for effective, fast, and computationally efficient strategies for robots to navigate and work in complex, real-world environments. The quest for fast methods began with classical approaches and evolved to learning-based methods. Recently, there has been a promising surge in applying physics-based machine learning approaches, which leverage physics principles and offer efficient solutions to high-dimensional motion planning and control problems.

This workshop will serve as an interdisciplinary, collaborative platform for researchers from motion planning, control, and computational mathematics to foster the exchange of insights and knowledge and develop practical, physics-driven methods for robot planning and control.
